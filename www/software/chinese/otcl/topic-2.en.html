Terminology [The included Chinese characters of this essay are encoded in big5.]

This topic is dedicated to the use of terminology in Chinese, plus relevant issues on the translated files. Just like English, Chinese has variants, and two major branches are so-called simplified and traditional Chinese. Although variations exist, sitll I regard all of them Chinese language, basically. The reason is clear:

Every human language is a diversity in unity. The diversity springs naturally from the way different human beings use the language. Each of us is unique — slightly different from every one of our fellows — and consequently we each use our language also in a slightly different way from anyone else. The unity comes from our use of language to communicate with one another. As long as we talk and write among ourselves, the language we use can vary only within understandable bounds; it has to be basically the same for all of us.
John Algeo
A Paradox: Diversity and Unity
The English Language: Variation, the Dictionary, and the User
Webster's New Wourld Dictionary -Third College Edition

We recognize that long-term separation would cause a language to diverse into two — just like English and German, but this situation doesn't happen in Chinese yet. Since Chinese is still a "Write the Same" language, it's not likely to occur in the next five hundred years in my opinion.

GENERAL ISSUE ON THE TRANSLATED FILES

Here are some notable reminders for us to prepare both simplified and traditional texts. Inter-translating encoding system between big5 and gb2312, please be using iconv.

postfix of file name
"cn" and "zh" are used to denote simplified and traditional pages, respectively.
encoding system used
"gb2312" and "big5" are used in simplified and traditional pages, respectively.
explicitly specify encoding system in GNU/Emacs
;;; DO NOT PUT THIS ON ZHS OR ZHT FILES...
;;; Local Variables:
;;; coding: chinese-big5
;;; End:
If you are editing gb2312 encoded file, the coding line should be replaced by
;;; coding: chinese-iso-8bit

RECORDED ZH2-DIFF AND GENERAL TERMINOLOGY

Contributors of this section [You will be listed if your suggestion is recorded, if not, please do inform me about that]: Chao-Hong Liu, Royal Bai, Syue-Ping Ma, Qingjiu Zhu, Bin Zhang, Yang Song.

The term "zh2-diff" is what I used to denote the different translatings of simplified and traditional Chinese. One thing I should be emphasizing is that these differences mean nothing to the language itself, and in fact I found that in many cases they are both good translatings. The situation is only another instance of Murphy's law: Where there is a possibility, there will be a reality. And it also shows how flexible Chinese the language is.

However, this section records not only the zh2-diff but also other general terminology. It's always of my faith that we should keep pushing to the front in translating, therefore I don't really care about what terminology we are using today, and if there is a better one, go ahead to use it and state your reasons here in this topic by sending emails to me.

I should be telling you that "my" language is no longer traditional nor simplified Chinese, and I always have to modify my texts to look "right" to a traditional Chinese reader or a simplified one. Therefore if you believe that my language is not proper to one of the two variants of Chinese, please be informing about that, and I will record your suggestions here.

Besides sending your recordings of zh2-diff to me, you should also put them inside the translated essay. This information is useful whenever we would like to modify it. Example could be found in source html of topic-1.zh.html such as:

CLOSING

Be ambitious and be brave if you have your translatings adequate to Chinese language! We should bear in mind that most computer terminology we use today doesn't even exist in these decades, so why should we insist so much on some? Furhtermore, I wish we GNU/CTT members would take the lead in translating the ideas of the field of computer science but not only GNU project. And if you have any question on your work, we may always discuss on our mailing lists.
