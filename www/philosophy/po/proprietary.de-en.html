Proprietary Software

Proprietary software, nonfree software, is software that doesn't respect users' freedom and community. The developer or owner has power over its users. That in itself is an injustice, but it leads to more injustice. Power corrupts, so the program's owner often takes advantage of it to do further nasty things to the users.

  • Proprietary back doors
  • Proprietary insecurity
  • Proprietary sabotage
  • Proprietary surveillance
  • Digital Restrictions Management

The way to avoid these forms of abuse is by insisting on free (freedom-respecting) software. Since free software is controlled by its users, they have a chance to defend themselves and each other against malicious software functions.
